Naan The Indian Flat Bread

Authentic Indian naan

Description

Naan is a leavened, oven-baked flatbread that's a staple in South Asian cuisine. Unlike pita bread, which puffs up into a pocket during baking, naan stays flat. The dough is typically enriched with yogurt, ghee (clarified butter), or milk, giving it a softer and richer texture than pita. Traditionally, naan is cooked in a tandoor, a cylindrical clay oven heated by charcoal or wood. The intense heat of the tandoor cooks the naan quickly, charring the surface and creating a smoky flavor.

You can enjoy naan in many ways. It's perfect for scooping up curries and stews, soaking up flavorful sauces, or using as a base for mini pizzas. Naan can also be stuffed with savory fillings like vegetables or cheese, making it a satisfying hand-held meal.

Ingredients

  • ⅔ cup warm water (110 degrees F/45 degrees C)
  • 1 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon white sugar
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons plain yogurt
  • ¼ cup ghee
  • 2 teaspoons onion seeds

Steps

  1. Whisk warm water with yeast and sugar until yeast is dissolved. Cover and let stand in a warm place for 10 minutes.
  2. Sift flour and salt three times into a large bowl. Add yeast mixture, yogurt, and 1/2 of the ghee. Mix into a soft dough, then knead on a floured surface until dough is smooth and elastic, about 5 minutes. Place dough into a large greased bowl, cover, and let stand in a warm place for 1 1/2 hours or until doubled in volume.
  3. Punch down dough, then knead for 5 minutes. Divide dough into six equal pieces. Roll each piece out into 8-inch rounds.
  4. Cover an oven tray with foil and grease the foil. Brush dough rounds with remaining ghee and sprinkle with kalonji. Cook one at a time under the broiler until puffed and lightly browned, about 2 minutes per side.
